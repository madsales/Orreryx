Where Is Gaza on the Map?

Gaza Strip — Location & Geography

The Gaza Strip is a narrow coastal territory on the southeastern coast of the Mediterranean Sea. It borders Israel to the north and east, Egypt (Sinai Peninsula) to the south at the Rafah crossing, and the Mediterranean Sea to the west.

Key locations within Gaza tracked by Orreryx: Gaza City (north), Khan Yunis (south-central), Rafah (southern border), the Erez crossing (north, with Israel), and the Kerem Shalom crossing (southeast, main goods entry point).

Market Impact of the Israel-Gaza War

Oil & Energy

Brent Crude Spiked

Fears of wider Middle East escalation pushed Brent oil up sharply. Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping disrupted 12% of global trade, increasing energy transport costs.

Defence Stocks

Elbit, Raytheon, LMT Up

Israeli defence company Elbit Systems, plus US and European defence contractors, surged as weapons orders accelerated. Iron Dome demand increased globally.

Shipping & Trade

Red Sea Route Disrupted

Houthi attacks in Yemen (in response to Gaza war) forced shipping rerouting around Africa, adding 10-14 days and significant cost to Europe-Asia freight.

Safe Havens

Gold & USD Elevated

Gold prices rose above $2,400/oz. USD strengthened as investors sought safe-haven assets amid Middle East instability and risk-off sentiment.

When did the Israel-Gaza war start?
The current war began on October 7, 2023, when Hamas launched a major attack into southern Israel, killing approximately 1,200 people and taking around 250 hostages. Israel launched a military campaign in Gaza in response. The conflict has caused tens of thousands of casualties and one of the worst humanitarian crises in the region's history.

What is the Gaza Strip?
The Gaza Strip is a 365 km² coastal Palestinian territory bordered by Israel (north and east), Egypt (south) and the Mediterranean Sea (west). With ~2.3 million people it is one of the most densely populated areas on Earth. Governed by Hamas since 2007, it has been under an Israeli and Egyptian blockade since then.
