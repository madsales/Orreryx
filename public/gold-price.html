Why Does Gold Rise During Wars?

Gold's safe-haven status in wartime stems from five structural properties that no paper asset can replicate:

🛡️

No Counterparty Risk

Physical gold cannot default, be inflated away or frozen by sanctions. When governments and banks are suspected of instability, gold is the only asset with zero counterparty risk.

🌍

Universal Acceptance

Gold is accepted as a store of value in every country on earth, making it uniquely useful during crises that disrupt normal currency convertibility or cross-border capital flows.

💰

Central Bank Demand

Central banks in BRICS nations have been buying gold at record pace since 2022 — over 1,000 tonnes per year — as de-dollarisation accelerates. This structural bid provides a price floor.

📈

Inflation Hedge

Wars cause deficit spending and monetary expansion — both inflationary. Gold has preserved purchasing power across every currency debasement episode in history.

🔒

Limited Supply

All the gold ever mined totals approximately 205,000 tonnes. Annual mine production of 3,500 tonnes cannot be meaningfully accelerated even at higher prices, constraining supply permanently.

⚡

Liquidity in Crisis

The global gold market trades $180 billion per day — more than most major stock exchanges. Gold can be liquidated instantly even when equity markets are closed or illiquid.

What Is Driving the Gold Price in 2026?

Multiple structural and cyclical factors are converging to support gold prices at historically elevated levels in 2026:

1. Geopolitical Polycrisis. The simultaneous occurrence of the Russia-Ukraine war, the Israel-Gaza conflict, US-China tensions over Taiwan, and North Korean missile escalation has created the highest concurrent geopolitical risk since the Cold War. Each flashpoint independently supports a gold price premium; together, they create a structural bid that does not dissipate between individual events.

2. De-Dollarisation & Central Bank Buying. Sanctions on Russia's central bank reserves in 2022 triggered a global reassessment of dollar-denominated reserve assets. BRICS nations, led by China, India, and Gulf states, have dramatically increased gold reserve accumulation. The People's Bank of China alone added over 300 tonnes in 2023–2024. This structural demand shift is unlike anything seen since Bretton Woods collapsed in 1971.

3. Federal Reserve Policy Pivot. As the US Federal Reserve moves toward rate cuts in response to slowing growth, the real yield on US Treasuries falls — directly reducing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold. Historically, every major Fed easing cycle has been accompanied by a sustained gold bull market.

4. US Fiscal Concerns. The US federal debt exceeding $36 trillion, combined with structural deficits above $1.5 trillion per year, has reignited concerns about long-term dollar purchasing power. Gold benefits from any narrative that questions the dollar's store-of-value properties — and that narrative is increasingly mainstream across institutional investors.

5. Retail and ETF Demand Recovery. After two years of ETF outflows as rates rose, gold ETF holdings are recovering as institutional and retail investors return. Simultaneously, Asian retail demand — particularly in China and India — is at multi-year highs as local currency weakness makes gold an even more attractive inflation and devaluation hedge.

Frequently Asked Questions — Gold Price

What is the gold price today per ounce?
The current gold spot price is displayed live at the top of this page, updated every 15 seconds. It is derived from the SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) ETF price multiplied by 10.43, which closely tracks the LBMA official gold price published twice daily from London. Gold is priced in US dollars per troy ounce (1 troy oz = 31.1 grams).
Why does gold price go up during wars and geopolitical crises?
Gold rises during wars and crises because it is the world's oldest safe-haven asset — it cannot be defaulted on, inflated away, or frozen by sanctions. During Russia's 2022 Ukraine invasion, gold surged 8.7% in two weeks. During COVID-19, it rose 25% in six months. Every major conflict since World War II has produced a measurable gold price spike as capital flees risk assets.
What is the difference between gold spot price and gold futures price?
The gold spot price is the current price for immediate delivery of one troy ounce. Gold futures prices represent agreed prices for future delivery — typically 1, 3, or 6 months forward — and include a cost-of-carry premium. The spot price is what physical bullion, ETFs like GLD, and most retail gold products are priced against. Futures are used by miners to hedge future production revenue.
How is the gold spot price calculated?
The international gold spot price is set continuously 24 hours a day on the London OTC market. The LBMA publishes an official gold benchmark at 10:30am and 3pm London time, determined by an electronic auction among accredited participants. The GLD ETF tracks spot gold closely — each share represents approximately 0.0958 troy ounces, giving a conversion factor of approximately 10.43x to the spot price.
What are the best gold mining stocks to buy?
The most widely traded gold mining stocks are Newmont (NEM), Barrick Gold (GOLD), and Wheaton Precious Metals (WPM) for large-cap exposure. The VanEck Gold Miners ETF (GDX) provides diversified large-cap miner exposure, while GDXJ covers junior miners with higher volatility. Mining stocks typically offer 2–3× leveraged exposure to gold price moves due to their fixed cost structures, but carry operational and jurisdictional risk that physical gold does not. This is not financial advice.
Will the gold price go up in 2026?
Major bank gold price forecasts for 2026 are broadly bullish, driven by elevated geopolitical risk, record central bank buying (1,000+ tonnes/year since 2022), de-dollarisation by BRICS nations, and potential Federal Reserve rate cuts reducing opportunity costs of holding gold. Goldman Sachs, UBS and Bank of America have all raised gold price targets above $3,000/oz for 2026. However, gold prices can fall sharply if risk appetite recovers or real yields rise unexpectedly.
How does the Federal Reserve affect gold prices?
The Fed influences gold through interest rate policy and the US dollar. Rate hikes increase the opportunity cost of non-yielding gold and strengthen the dollar (making gold more expensive in other currencies), typically suppressing prices. Rate cuts do the opposite — reducing yields and weakening the dollar, boosting gold. This is why every major Fed easing cycle since 1971 has coincided with a gold bull market.
What is the all-time high gold price?
Gold surpassed $3,000 per troy ounce in 2025, setting a new nominal all-time high driven by central bank buying, US fiscal concerns, and geopolitical instability. The previous record was approximately $2,080/oz in August 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic. Adjusted for inflation, the 1980 gold peak of $850/oz — reached during the oil crisis, Soviet invasion of Afghanistan and Iranian hostage crisis — equates to over $3,500 in 2026 dollars, meaning gold is approaching but has not yet definitively exceeded its inflation-adjusted all-time high.
