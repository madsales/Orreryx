Why Silver Demand Is Structural — Industrial Uses

Unlike gold, over 50% of silver demand is industrial. This means silver participates in both the monetary safe-haven trade AND the global industrial cycle — amplifying moves in both directions.

☀️
Solar Photovoltaics
~20g per panel · 200M+ oz/yr demand

Silver paste conducts electricity in solar cells. Every GW of solar capacity requires approximately 0.7 million ounces of silver. The IEA expects 500+ GW/yr installations by 2030.

⚡
Electric Vehicles
~25–50g per EV · fast-growing

Silver is used in EV charging contacts, battery management systems, power electronics, and LIDAR sensors. Rising EV penetration creates compounding annual silver demand growth.

📡
5G Infrastructure
~5g per base station · millions deploying

5G base stations require silver-coated contacts and RF components due to silver's unmatched conductivity. Global 5G rollout represents a multi-year demand driver.

🛡️
Defence Electronics
Classified volume · war-driven spike

Missiles, radar systems, communications equipment, and drone electronics all require silver. Defence budget expansions in NATO and Indo-Pacific nations translate directly into silver demand.

💊
Medical & Antimicrobial
~50M oz/yr globally

Silver's antimicrobial properties make it essential in wound dressings, medical devices, and hospital surfaces. Post-pandemic awareness has permanently increased medical silver consumption.

🔋
Next-Gen Batteries
Silver-zinc · solid-state prototypes

Silver-zinc batteries offer 40% higher energy density than lithium-ion. Military and aerospace applications are adopting them now; consumer adoption would create explosive demand.

Silver Price FAQ

Common questions about silver spot price, silver ETFs, and silver's role in geopolitical risk portfolios.

What is the silver spot price today?
The silver spot price is the current market price for immediate delivery of one troy ounce of .999 fine silver. Orreryx derives the live silver spot price from SLV ETF data (multiplied by ~1.067) and updates it every 15 seconds. Check the live price widget at the top of this page for the current figure.
Why does silver price rise during wars and geopolitical crises?
Silver benefits from two simultaneous forces during crises: safe-haven monetary demand (like gold) and an industrial demand spike for defence electronics, solar panels, and communications equipment. The Russia-Ukraine war triggered an 18% silver surge over 3 months as these forces converged.
What is the gold/silver ratio and what does it signal?
The gold/silver ratio measures how many ounces of silver buy one ounce of gold. The historical average is ~60:1. Ratios above 80:1 historically signal silver is undervalued versus gold. At the COVID peak in March 2020, the ratio hit 124:1 — just before silver surged 141%.
Is SLV a good proxy for the silver spot price?
Yes. SLV (iShares Silver Trust) holds physical silver and each share represents ~0.9372 troy ounces. Multiplying the SLV price by ~1.067 gives a close approximation of the silver spot price per ounce. SLV tracks spot very closely, though it may trade at a small premium or discount during extreme volatility.
What is silver's all-time high price?
Silver's nominal all-time high was $49.51/oz in April 2011 during QE-era commodity supercycle momentum. The 1980 Hunt Brothers squeeze hit $49.45 in nominal terms, which exceeds $170/oz in inflation-adjusted terms — illustrating how far silver remains from its inflation-adjusted all-time high.
How does Federal Reserve policy affect silver?
Silver is highly sensitive to real interest rates. When the Fed raises rates, the dollar strengthens and silver falls as the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding metals rises. The 2022 hiking cycle caused a 30% silver decline. Conversely, rate cuts or dovish signals typically drive silver up — often with 2–3× the leverage of gold.
What is the difference between silver price and silver futures?
The silver spot price is for immediate delivery. Silver futures contracts (COMEX SI) allow traders to lock in a price for delivery at a future date — typically 1, 3, or 6 months out. Futures prices include a "contango" premium for storage and financing costs. When the futures price is below spot ("backwardation"), it often signals very tight near-term physical supply.
