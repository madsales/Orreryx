Russia Ukraine War — Current Frontline Situation 2026

As of 2026, the Russia Ukraine war has entered a prolonged attritional phase. Russia controls approximately 18% of Ukrainian territory, including occupied portions of four oblasts and the Crimean Peninsula. Ukrainian forces have demonstrated the capability to strike deep into Russian territory using long-range drones while defending along a 1,000km frontline.

Russian offensives continue primarily in Donetsk Oblast, with grinding advances around key population centres. The Zaporizhzhia nuclear plant remains under Russian control and continues to pose an escalation risk. Kherson Oblast sees regular cross-Dnipro river exchanges. In the north, the Sumy and Kharkiv oblasts face continuing cross-border pressure.

Ukraine has significantly expanded its drone warfare capability, conducting regular strikes on Russian oil infrastructure, military logistics hubs and — controversially — targets within Russia's recognised pre-2014 borders. These strikes have forced Russia to redirect substantial air defence resources to protect its own territory, degrading coverage over frontline zones. Western military aid continues through NATO channels, though political support has become more variable as the war enters its fourth year.

Russia Ukraine War Timeline — Key Dates

Understanding the Ukraine war map requires context on how the conflict evolved. These are the pivotal moments from the full-scale invasion to the current frontline:

FEB 24, 2022
Full-Scale Invasion Begins. Russia launches simultaneous attacks from Belarus toward Kyiv, from Crimea into southern Ukraine, and across the eastern border. Ukrainian forces halt the Kyiv thrust within weeks.
MAR–APR 2022
Kyiv Defended, Bucha Discovered. Russian forces withdraw from northern Ukraine. Evidence of atrocities in Bucha and Irpin triggers international sanctions escalation and increased weapons deliveries.
SEP 2022
Ukrainian Counter-Offensive. Ukraine liberates Kharkiv Oblast in a rapid offensive, retaking thousands of square kilometres. Russia responds with illegal annexation declarations of four oblasts and partial mobilisation.
NOV 2022
Kherson City Liberated. Russian forces withdraw from the west bank of the Dnipro and Kherson city — the only regional capital Russia had captured — is reclaimed by Ukraine.
FEB–MAY 2023
Battle of Bakhmut. Russia's Wagner Group captures Bakhmut after months of the war's bloodiest urban fighting. Ukraine describes the battle as a strategic grinder that degraded Russian assault forces.
JUN 2023
Kakhovka Dam Destroyed. The Nova Kakhovka hydroelectric dam is breached, flooding vast areas of Kherson Oblast and displacing tens of thousands. Ukraine and Russia blame each other.
2024
Drone Warfare Escalates. Ukraine launches sustained long-range drone campaigns against Russian oil refineries, military infrastructure and — for the first time — targets in Moscow. Russia escalates Shahed drone attacks on Ukrainian cities and energy grid.
AUG 2024
Kursk Incursion. Ukraine crosses into Kursk Oblast, Russia — the first foreign military occupation of Russian territory since WW2. The operation forces Russia to redeploy from the Donetsk front.
2025–2026
Attritional Stalemate & Negotiations. The front lines stabilise in a grinding war of attrition. International diplomatic pressure intensifies for a ceasefire framework. Orreryx tracks all developments in real time on the live Ukraine war map.
