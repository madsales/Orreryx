# OrreryX — Copywriting Skills

> Reference for headlines, landing page copy, ad copy, and any written marketing asset.

---

## Core Copywriting Framework

**PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)**
1. Name the exact pain
2. Twist the knife — make it feel urgent
3. Present OrreryX as the only logical answer

**Example:**
> By the time mainstream news covers a geopolitical event, the trade has already moved. OrreryX gives you the signal before the market does — live conflict data, instant market impact, $14.99/month.

---

## Headline Formulas

**Contrast headline:** "[What others charge] vs. [what we charge]"
> Bloomberg charges $2,000/month. OrreryX is $14.99.

**Speed headline:** "Know [outcome] before [the crowd]"
> Know why oil spiked before your broker does.

**Specificity headline:** Use exact numbers, not vague claims
> 13 live conflict zones. 40+ assets tracked. One dashboard.

**Curiosity headline:** Tease the "why" without revealing it
> Most investors missed what caused gold's 2.3% rally. Here's what OrreryX showed.

**Direct value headline:**
> Geopolitical intelligence. Real-time. $14.99/month.

---

## Landing Page Hierarchy

1. **Hero:** Bold claim + 1-line subhead + CTA
2. **Pain:** 2–3 sentences on what's broken without OrreryX
3. **Solution:** What OrreryX actually does (specific, not vague)
4. **Proof:** Numbers, comparisons, quotes
5. **Features:** 3–4 features with benefit framing ("not what it is, what it does for you")
6. **Pricing:** Simple, anchored against Bloomberg
7. **CTA:** Repeat hero CTA + "No credit card required"

## Words to Use

`know before`, `live conflict data`, `translate events into trades`, `war stocks`, `defense plays`, `safe-haven assets`, `oil spike`, `gold rally`, `market-moving event`, `stop being the last to know`, `one place for all the signals`, `geopolitical risk → market impact`

## Words to Avoid

`cutting-edge`, `paradigm`, `synergy`, `disruptive`, `robust`, `comprehensive`, `leverage` (verb), `innovative`, `world-class`, `best-in-class`, `seamless`

---

## Copy Length Guidelines

| Asset | Length |
|-------|--------|
| Twitter post | 1–3 lines (no thread) |
| Twitter thread | 5–7 tweets |
| LinkedIn post | 150–300 words |
| Email | 100–200 words body |
| Ad headline | 25–30 characters (Google), 70 (LinkedIn) |
| Ad description | 80–90 characters (Google), 150 (LinkedIn) |
| Hero headline | 6–10 words |
| Hero subhead | 1 sentence, max 20 words |

---

## Self-Review Checklist

Before finalizing any copy:
- [ ] Does the first sentence make someone want to read the second?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA?
- [ ] Are there any banned words?
- [ ] Is there a specific number or proof point?
- [ ] Does it sound like a human wrote it?
- [ ] Could you cut 20% without losing meaning?
