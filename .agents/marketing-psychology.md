# OrreryX — Marketing Psychology Skills

> Cognitive principles applied to OrreryX pricing, copy, UX, and conversion flows.

---

## 1. Anchoring

Set the reference price high, then reveal yours.

**Application:**
- Always mention Bloomberg ($2,000+/month) or Bloomberg terminal rate first
- Then reveal: "OrreryX starts at $0.99/month"
- Pricing page: show Command ($34.99) before Analyst ($14.99) before Starter ($0.99)

**Email example:**
> "Hedge funds pay $349/month for slower, weekly data. We're $14.99."

---

## 2. Loss Aversion

People fear losing more than they value gaining. Frame around what they miss without OrreryX.

**Application:**
- "By the time Bloomberg covers it, the trade has moved"
- "Stop being the last to know"
- D14 retention email: "You're missing live signals right now"
- Cancel save-offer: "You'll lose real-time access and email alerts"

**Do:** Frame inaction as a cost, not just a missed benefit.

---

## 3. Decoy Effect

Add a middle option that makes the target tier look like the obvious choice.

**Application:**
- Starter ($0.99): stripped down — no real-time, no alerts
- **Analyst ($14.99): target tier** — real-time + alerts + AI analysis
- Command ($34.99): premium — risk map + unlimited AI + exports

The gap between Starter and Analyst should feel small. The gap between Analyst and "free news" should feel huge.

---

## 4. Goal-Gradient Effect

People accelerate toward a goal as they get closer.

**Application:**
- Onboarding progress: "You're 1 step away from live data"
- Referral program: "2 of 3 referrals done — 1 more = free month"
- Trial countdown: "2 days left in your free trial"
- Show what they've already unlocked, not what's locked

---

## 5. Endowment Effect

People overvalue what they already own.

**Application:**
- Personalize the dashboard to feel like "their" risk map
- "Your watchlist" > "The watchlist"
- Save their preferences, show their most-viewed conflicts
- Cancel flow: "You'll lose your saved alerts and watchlist"

---

## 6. Social Proof

People copy what similar people do.

**Application:**
- "Join 12,000+ investors tracking geopolitical risk" (update number)
- "Traders who use OrreryX before market open outperform..." (if data exists)
- Show logos: "Featured in [press]" if coverage exists
- Reddit/community: authentic testimonials > polished quotes

**Rule:** Specific > vague. "12,347 investors" beats "thousands of investors".

---

## 7. Scarcity and Urgency

Real scarcity (limited trial period) > manufactured scarcity.

**Application:**
- "Free trial: 3 days" — real, not fake
- Trial expiry email: "Your free trial ends in 24 hours"
- Do NOT use fake countdown timers. It erodes trust.
- Do NOT say "limited spots" unless literally true.

---

## 8. Authority and Credibility

**Application:**
- Compare favorably to Bloomberg, Reuters, Stratfor — named specifically
- "Aggregated from Reuters feeds and verified global sources"
- Founder/team voice in emails: direct, personal, not corporate
- Pricing page: "The same intelligence hedge funds pay thousands for"

---

## 9. Reciprocity

Give value first, then ask.

**Application:**
- Free tier gives real value (not crippled)
- Day-3 email delivers insight (oil/conflict context) before upsell
- Lead magnets: "Get the geopolitical risk briefing free"
- Community posts: genuine analysis, OrreryX mention is secondary

---

## 10. Cognitive Ease

Reduce friction. Simpler = more trusted.

**Application:**
- No credit card for free trial
- Magic link login (no password)
- Pricing: 3 tiers max, clear names (Starter / Analyst / Command)
- Onboarding: one action at a time, not a wall of features
- Subject lines: 2–4 words, lowercase

---

## Hierarchy of Principles (Apply in This Order)

1. Loss aversion — in all retention/churn copy
2. Anchoring — in all pricing/comparison copy
3. Decoy effect — in pricing page design
4. Goal-gradient — in onboarding + referral flows
5. Social proof — wherever credibility matters
6. Reciprocity — in all value-first content
