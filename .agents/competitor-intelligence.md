# OrreryX — Competitor Intelligence

> How OrreryX positions against competitors and what to monitor.

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Tier 1: Direct Competitors

**Bloomberg Terminal**
- Price: $2,000+/month ($24,000+/year)
- Target: Institutional, professional traders
- Weakness: Overkill for retail, prohibitively expensive, no real-time geopolitical risk mapping
- OrreryX angle: "Same signal. 1/100th the price."

**Reuters Eikon (now LSEG Workspace)**
- Price: $1,800–$3,600/month
- Target: Professional financial media and analysts
- Weakness: Slow, text-heavy, complex UI, institutional only
- OrreryX angle: Speed + market translation + retail-accessible

**Stratfor (RANE Network)**
- Price: $349+/month
- Target: Corporate security, government, enterprise
- Weakness: Weekly cadence, academic tone, no real-time market impact data
- OrreryX angle: "Stratfor tells you what happened. OrreryX tells you what moves."

### Tier 2: Partial Competitors

**Twitter/X geopolitics accounts** (e.g. @IntelCrab, @WarMonitor)
- Free
- Weakness: No structured data, no market translation, noise-heavy
- OrreryX angle: "OrreryX organizes the signal. Twitter gives you the noise."

**Google News / Apple News**
- Free
- Weakness: Zero financial context, algorithmic noise
- OrreryX angle: "Free news tells you what happened. OrreryX tells you what to do."

**ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data)**
- Free/academic
- Weakness: Data-only, no market correlation, no UI for investors
- OrreryX angle: We use conflict data + translate it for investors

**Geopolitical Futures (George Friedman)**
- Price: ~$40/month
- Target: Geopolitical analysts, policy readers
- Weakness: Slow, long-form analysis, no live market data, no actionable signals
- OrreryX angle: We're faster, live, and market-connected

## Competitive Monitoring

**What to watch:**
- New geopolitical data startups (Product Hunt, BetaList launches)
- Bloomberg pricing changes or retail product launches
- Stratfor/RANE product updates
- New Twitter/X features (competitor to free social content)

**Frequency:** Monthly scan. Flag immediately if a direct competitor launches a retail product or significantly drops price.

---

## Win Themes (When Pitching Against Competitors)

1. **Price:** "Bloomberg is $2,000/month. We're $14.99."
2. **Real-time:** "Stratfor is weekly. OrreryX is live."
3. **Translation:** "Reuters gives you the event. OrreryX gives you the trade."
4. **Accessibility:** "Bloomberg is for institutions. OrreryX is for you."
5. **Specificity:** "Twitter gives you noise. OrreryX gives you which assets move."

## What NOT to Do Competitively

- Don't attack competitors by name in paid ads (policy violations)
- Don't make claims you can't prove ("most accurate data")
- Don't underestimate free alternatives — Twitter is a real competitor for attention
- Don't copy competitor pricing without understanding their unit economics
